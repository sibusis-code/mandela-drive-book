Why reading changes everything

A child who cannot read is a future waiting to be unlocked.

In too many South African classrooms, there are no books to take home, no library to get lost in, no story to call your own.

The reading crisis is not about ability — it is about access. Talent is spread evenly across every township and village; opportunity is not. When a child holds their first real book, something shifts. Confidence grows. Imagination wakes up. A future that once felt closed begins to open.

We are not here to dwell on the problem. We are here to hand a child the next chapter of their life — one book, one shelf, one school at a time.

“A library was the spark. It said to me: there is a bigger world, and you belong in it.”

Our impact

From a single classroom to schools across the country.

What began as one Mandela Day book drive has grown into a movement of readers, volunteers and partners.

2021

The first drive

Our first Mandela Day book drive delivered hundreds of books to a Gauteng school — and saw the now-iconic “Harry Potter spotted” moment of pure joy.

2022

Shelves, not just books

Volunteers painted and built colourful reading corners, turning bare classrooms into spaces children wanted to be in.

2023

Reading together

We added reading circles and mentorship — pairing volunteers with learners so books are opened, not just delivered.

Today

A growing movement

35+ schools, 12,000+ children and a community of corporate partners, families and book lovers writing the next chapter together.
